Diatoms are Everywhere

Posted by Janice on February 17, 2016

There’s an interest in diatoms in the Foldscope community. Did you know that diatoms can be found not only in pond water, but also in salt water, in the soil, on rocks, in the Arctic and Antarctic, in hydrothermal vents, and in any moist environment no matter how small? Did you know that diatoms are used in forensic science, are found in ancient clays used in making vessels, diatomaceous earth, toothpaste, and cleansers? See if you can collect diatoms from different places and view them with your Foldscope.

I am a researcher that studies diatoms, and I would be glad to share what I know. What have you found using your Foldscope?

      Hi Manu,

      There are many identification “keys” available–they’re not the usual dichotomous keys because the classification and phylogenetics of diatoms at the family, class, and order levels has not been worked out. Diatoms are Chromists if you are a phycologist or Stramenopiles if your a protozoologist. Diatoms are heterokonts or bacillariophytes at the phylum or class level (depending on who you ask), but beyond that, one has to go to the literature about particular genus-species designations. Another item to consider is that if one uses “guides” for diatom identifications for North America, those identifications may not be the same, say, for European or Asian diatoms. Endemism is ubiquitous in the diatom world, so, for example, some diatom species from Lake Hovsgol, Mongolia will not be the same as those species from Lake Michigan for a given genus, even though the two taxa look very similar. The environment where diatoms are collected tells us much about identifications.

      Having said this, some websites for diatom identifications include:

      https://westerndiatoms.colorado.edu/
      http://craticula.ncl.ac.uk/EADiatomKey/html/
      http://www.umich.edu/~phytolab/GreatLakesDiatomHomePage/top.html
      http://rbg-web2.rbge.org.uk/ADIAC/pubdat/pubdat.html

      As I come up with other websites, I’ll list them.
