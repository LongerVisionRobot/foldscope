    Miniature insects are under represented since they are so small (smaller than head of a pin) and they get overlooked when compared to bigger cousins.

    Now, I got a few hints in your videos; on which basis – I am very sure I know the answer of what rough species this belongs to. Here are observations I made:
    1) When you came close to it with a forceps; the insect literally jumped away and flew.
    2) In your macroscopic images (very low mag), I noticed that the wing has a pointed shape in the end (almost like a flower petal)
    3) They are very hairy under the microscope.. which is a very interesting reason – since for small things to fly, they need to increase the aerodynamic drag – and they do so by adding hair (since the wing is too small to generate lift forces to fly).

    But before I share my hypothesis; I would like you to add two more videos –
    a) of just the wing and
    b) the antenna itself.

    Many insects are identified by venation of the wing patterns. Most mosquitoes can be exactly identified by looking at the wing venation patterns (yes insect wings have little veins to transport insect blood).

    Now, what is enormously exciting for me; in parallel I noticed the exact same insect in my bathroom in California; as the one you show here in Delhi. It’s a tiny little thing – but it is spread across the planet. How fascinating!! I will make the post of my data and we will compare notes to see if it actually is the same species – which will be wonderful.

    I am excited to see if the two species across the world match.
