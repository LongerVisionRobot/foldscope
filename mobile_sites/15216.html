    Thanks Saad! And thanks to the department for this wonderful oportunity for our students to explore!

    We set out to find these animals because we have been researching their unique abilities. We were surprised to find they are all around us if you know where to look.

    The first step we took was to have all students go out with zip lock bags and gather moss and lichen as a homework assignment. We wanted to get enough samples to increase our chances of finding one.

    Next, we took our samples and placed them in large flasks filled with spring water and allowed the sample to sit for several hours covered.

    After this, we took our pipettes and prepared various slide samples one drop each. At first we found nothing, just plant cells and particles. So one student suggested we might better isolate the waterbear if we agitate the sample taken directly from the moss.

    It seemed to have worked!

    The 5th sample collected directly from the moss contained one waterbear!

    In all we took over 20 samples and found 1!!

    I believe that most of our samples being gathered after a light rain may have helped in our discovery, since we did not have to wait long for possible reanimation.

    Our students are actually presenting this project as well as a demonstration of foldscope and it’s real life applications at the Louisiana S.T.E.M. Expo. Competition!

    Wish us luck and good luck to all!

    Hope this was helpful!

    Ken,
