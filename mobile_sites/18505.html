Drosophila

Posted by Sawant Maithili on August 30, 2016

Hey guys! Always wanted to observe Drosophila (fruit fly) , but couldn’t catch one for days. Now found a convenient method. Caught the fly in a jar and kept it for a week. The fly doesn’t crush as when we try to catch it and can be easily observed under our foldscope! 🙂

      Firstly thank you sir for conducting such an awesome workshop on foldscope in Ruia. Loved the new techinques by which we can view and snap the microbial world. It was initially really tough focusing on foldscope, but your simple innovation helped me out a lot.
      Now about capturing the fruit fly, it was also initially really tidious for me to catch hold of the small creatures. But later i thought of an idea…i kept a small rotten banana for few hours undisturbed, a large number of flies were seen after a while. Then i took a flat medium sized jar in which the flies can easily be caught up. Just dragged the jar over gently above the fruit. Around 4 flies were easily caught up in the container. Immediately placed the lid on. Kept the jar for around a week and all the fruit flies were intact but had died. Ensure not to take a large container, it will take longer time for the flies to die. Later removed a tiny one of them and placed it on sticky tape slide which was provided with the foldscope. The rest of observation is all done by the simplicity of our foldscope. Working with a light micoscope couldn’t had given the great results like foldscope! 🙂
