    Dear Guru and Shree,

    Wonderful observations. Absolutely delighted that you are documenting the explorations – and inspiring many others.

    A few suggestions:
    1) Try aligning the magnetic tape to the cellphone really well – so you will get a complete field of view (so you see a full circle).
    2) You can digitally zoom in a little and focus with that – it will give you sharper focus.
    3) You should carefully study this post from me: https://microcosmos.foldscope.com/?p=16093 which allows you to do focus locking and field of view locking. It’s useful to be able to make modifications so you can do long term imaging of live things. See video in that post.
    4) Next, you should also add a diffuser (just a piece of paper will do) to make a more diffused illumination source. Read this post carefully to see the value of illumination: https://microcosmos.foldscope.com/?p=9289
    5) Try some live imaging – it’s crucial to get a sense of live imaging with liquid samples. You will learn how to best mount and prepare live samples. Here is a post on how to watch individual bacteria: https://microcosmos.foldscope.com/?p=16549
    6) Assemble the high mag lens (the kit has a 450X lens already) carefully. It’s best to do this once you have gained confidence with the 150X lens. The assembly videos here provide the instructions: https://microcosmos.foldscope.com/?page_id=243
    7) I will share a few example datasets from the high mag lens and some tricks with the same as well.

    Please continue your explorations with intensity. It’s crucial to mix your observations with pre-prepared slides but also with things around you. Being able to go back and forth between what you do know and what you don’t know is fascinating. I am cheering for both of you.

    As you can tell, the community is as important as the tools itself. I want you to be leaders in this community; so you engage and inspire and mentor many others. This is like a multiplier effect; and we really need folks like you who share the passion of science broadly.

    More on this soon 🙂

    cheers
    manu
