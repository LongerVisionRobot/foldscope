Foldscope Fun At Point Lobos

Posted by dfabian on May 18, 2017

Hey there foldscope friends! I wanted to share my Point Lobos experience with you because there were lots of things to see and explore!

Point Lobos is a State Natural Reserve  located in Monterey County, California along the Central California coast. It is absolutely beautiful and people come from all over the world to get a glimpse of the breathtaking views and wildlife that can be found there. The reserve has beaches, tide pools, walking trails, etc.

  << Taken from Google Maps –> The green area jetting out into the water is Point Lobos.

I wanted to take a look at some of the things that I found using my foldscope. My favorite thing about the foldscope is that you use your phone as the viewing lense, which makes it super easy to snap pictures that you can share with others! Below are some of the foldscope pictures I took from the trip.

The four pictures below are foldscope pictures taken of a piece of a mole crab exoskeleton. In the bottom right image you can see the corner of the exoskeleton  that has little setae on the ends of it. A closeup of the setae can be seen in the image on the bottom left. Pretty awesome! I believe the top right image shows a few grains of sand that were on the exoskeleton.
