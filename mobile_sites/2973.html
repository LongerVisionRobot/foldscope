Small Jumping Spider

Posted by joeazzato on March 14, 2015

I was raking leaves when this spider found me.  I just had to look at it under the Foldscope!

I made a video as I moved it under the scope but the video was too large to upload, so here is the link to YouTube:

http://youtu.be/3QCz2IqbLVQ

This is from the Family Salticidae, I believe but there are more than 4000 species.  I have not narrowed it down beyond this I’m afraid.

 

The first four photos were taken with a jeweler’s loupe to show the whole spider and the size.

      Hi Jeneel,
      Thanks for your nice comments. I have been using an iPhone 6 Plus for the photos and videos. I tape the lens (m2) to it with scotch tape. have the camera on, and looking toward the light I move the lens into position and watch as the light through the m2 lens gets closer to the phone lens. I don’t press the tape down until it is aligned so I get most of the opening and as little of the edge as possible. I then put the slide in the Foldscope and line up the subject. Then I let the magnets connect and I can see the image on the iPhone screen. Then I move the slide or even the whole stage around to get different parts of the subject. I sometimes have to push the stage closer to the phone or pull it away to get a part focused. I take a lot of photos the ones that are not sharp get discarded. The LED light is a bit bright for the camera when mounted on the Foldscope so I built a small rig to support the camera with light under it. I will get a photo of that and post it.

      I am fascinated my the microscopic world all around us. I have some videos on YouTube that you might like. Here is a link to one of mosquito larvae taken with an iPad camera. https://youtu.be/kpODEFbWeDg
