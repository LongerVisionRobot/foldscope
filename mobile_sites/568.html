The foldscope came on Thursday and after 2 viewings of the videos, it was folded together.  It worked fine using ambient light and low power lens.  Then came to try the led light.  I am not sure if the condenser lens is necessary when using both the low power and high power lens.  When the low power lens is used it seems the light is too intense.  Also when the high power lens is used, the focus is tricky when used with cell phone camera.  Prepared slides are being used and the bone marrow sample worked well with the ambient light and low power lens.  The cell phone camera took an image of human ovary during ovulation.

More research will be done.

    Dear Richard,

    “I am not sure if the condenser lens is necessary when using both the low power and high power lens.

    Yes – you want to use condenser lens at all times when you use the light-module (PCB LED module). A condenser lens improves the imaging in a number of ways. You can do a simple test as well; to assemble and disassemble the same and collect some data to see the difference.

    “When the low power lens is used it seems the light is too intense.

    – This is again because of the condenser lens. Because of the aperture of the light source is mounted on the condenser lens; this is what regulates the amount of light. So you want to put the condenser lens. If you want to experiment; try putting different size holes to see the value of controlling inlet light into the sample.

    “Also when the high power lens is used, the focus is tricky when used with cell phone camera.

    – It’s important to have the cellphone camera be sitting on the edge of the table. The lens itself is 800 microns big and thus the back-focal length is small. To use the high-mag lens with the cell-phone camera; put the mobile phone on the side of a table. Taking data with videos allows you to go through multiple focal planes. Also, time-photo is another way to collect pictures since focus requires both hands.

    – From the picture you posted; and comparing it to other people’s pictures; something is not right. The best way to test something is to disassemble and see if you have all the parts in place. Specially the way you mounted the high-mag lens should be exactly as described in the video here.

    http://microcosmos.foldscope.com/assembly/

    Watch video #4 for cellphone data collection..

    Good luck. Play with different settings. The whole point is to play and see what works for your settings. Keep me in the loop via comments section..
