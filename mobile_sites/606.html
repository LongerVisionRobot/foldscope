Sugar crystals from my favorite Christmas drink

Posted by Manu Prakash on December 22, 2014

I love Christmas – and all the speciality food and drinks that come with it. While enjoying the “sugary” drink (that shall remain nameless in this post – let me just say I am in quebec and it’s very cold here); I just put a drop on a slide and let it dry.

To my surprise; I just saw these beautiful crystals. I am excited, not just because I love this “sugary” drink, but also how beautiful these crystals are. More on crystals coming soon – next I will try the classic citric acid crystal.

    That is a great question Niramay.

    Here is what I did; I made a thin smear out of my “sugar heavy” drink. But it’s other components also include liquor from oranges. As volatile compounds such as water and alcohol start to evaporate from the thin smear (I used a cover slip in this case) – the super-saturated solution of sugar will start to crystallize on any seeds/non-uniformities on the glass slide. Since forced the crystal between a cover slip and glass slide – the crystal can only grow in two dimensions – thus no 3D-crystals we are used to seeing.

    Straight lines naturally arise in crystals. Give this a try with any saturated solution; starting with salt mixed in water.
